# LRN — Learnings

**Core question:** What did I learn in the field?

Use this voice when you want to share credibility through experience — what you observed, what broke, and what changed your thinking.

:::info[Insight + LinkedIn]
Each Insight uses **one primary voice**. The paired **LinkedIn voice post** uses the **same voice** and **same tags** — one post per Insight, shorter format, link back to site.
:::

## When to use LRN

- You have firsthand experience with a problem or system
- Something surprised you or failed in an unexpected way
- You want to teach through story, not argument

## Structure

Follow this flow in order:

1. **Situation** — What you were working on or observing
2. **What broke / surprised you** — The moment reality diverged from expectation
3. **What you learned** — 3–5 specific bullets
4. **What changed in your thinking** — Your reframed takeaway

## Template

Copy this block and replace the bracketed placeholders:

```text
While working on [context or problem], one thing became clear:

[Unexpected observation]

What looked simple at first broke because:

* [Learning 1]
* [Learning 2]
* [Learning 3]

My biggest takeaway:

[Reframed lesson]
```

## Examples

### Example 1 — RAG

> While designing enterprise AI retrieval systems, one thing became clear: RAG creates confidence faster than correctness.
>
> What looked simple broke because:
>
> - Similarity was treated as truth
> - Policies were hidden inside prompts
> - Retrieval lacked identity awareness
> - Validation was missing
>
> **My biggest takeaway:** RAG improves access, but not authority.

### Example 2 — Agents

> While breaking down agentic workflows, one thing became clear: most failures are not model failures. They are system boundary failures.
>
> What repeatedly broke:
>
> - Agents owning decisions
> - Missing policy checkpoints
> - Tool calls without auditability
> - Context injected too late
>
> **My biggest takeaway:** Agent design is mostly architecture design.

## Pre-publish checklist

- [ ] Opens with a specific context — not a generic industry observation
- [ ] Names what actually broke or surprised you
- [ ] Lists 3–5 concrete learnings, not abstract advice
- [ ] Ends with one reframed takeaway
- [ ] Reads like experience, not a POV argument
