# G.A.I.N Types

Two classification layers for Gain Engine content — **pillars** (how G.A.I.N applies) and **Signature Articles** (which domain you map through G.A.I.N).

---

## G · A · I · N pillars

From [jitendersharma.dev/frameworks](https://jitendersharma.dev/frameworks):

| Slug | Letter | Pillar | Definition | Tag when the piece mainly proves… |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `grounded` | **G** | Grounded | Truth, context and knowledge alignment | RAG, retrieval, hallucinations, knowledge grounding |
| `adaptive` | **A** | Adaptive | Learning, feedback and continuous evolution | Evaluation, rollouts, feedback loops, what changed in the field |
| `intelligent` | **I** | Intelligent | Reasoning, agents and decision systems | Agents, MCP, decision models, policy-governed runtime |
| `native` | **N** | Native | Scalable, modular and future-ready design | Observability, cloud, production readiness, platform scale |

**Planning tables** may use the slug or the letter. Tag **one dominant G.A.I.N letter** on every Signature Article.

### G.A.I.N vs Core pillars

G.A.I.N is the **how** — not a fifth Core pillar. **Core pillars = where** (Insights only) → [Tagging Guidelines](../00 content/50 Tagging-Guidelines).

| Core pillar | Where you lead |
| --- | --- |
| Strategy & Architecture | Roadmaps, reference models, design authority |
| Platforms & Engineering | Cloud-native, observable, scalable systems |
| AI & Intelligence | Governed agents, RAG, production AI |
| Governance & Trust | Policy, compliance, operational resilience |
