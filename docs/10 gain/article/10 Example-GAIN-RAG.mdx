## One-line angle

RAG is a governed knowledge pipeline — not a vector database bolt-on.

## Opening thesis

Enterprise RAG fails when teams treat retrieval as the product. G.A.I.N RAG reframes RAG as a **grounded knowledge system** with explicit boundaries for identity, policy, and operational scale.

---

## Why this domain needs G.A.I.N

Most RAG advice stops at chunking and embedding. Enterprises need:

- **Truth alignment** — who can see what, and under which policy
- **Feedback loops** — eval harnesses that catch drift before users do
- **Decision boundaries** — when retrieval informs vs when deterministic systems decide
- **Production scale** — latency, cost, and observability as first-class design inputs

---

## Grounded (G)

**Patterns**

- Ingestion pipeline with source-of-truth metadata (owner, classification, TTL)
- Retrieval scoped by identity and policy — not global vector search
- Grounding layer that separates *candidate context* from *authorized context*

**Decisions**

- Chunk strategy follows document type — legal ≠ runbook ≠ ticket
- Re-ranking before generation — cheapest quality win most teams skip

**Anti-patterns**

- Treating top-k similarity as truth
- Skipping access control until the audit

---

## Adaptive (A)

- Offline eval sets per use case — not one generic benchmark
- Online feedback: thumbs, escalation, override events feed retrieval tuning
- Rollout gates: shadow mode → limited cohort → full production

---

## Intelligent (I)

- Orchestration decides *when* to retrieve, *what* to retrieve, and *whether* to answer
- Human-in-the-loop as an architecture decision — escalation paths designed in, not bolted on
- Agent tools call retrieval through governed APIs — not raw index access

---

## Native (N)

- Index lifecycle: rebuild, re-embed, deprecate — versioned like any data product
- Observability: log query, chunks retrieved, policy decisions, generation metadata
- Cost controls: cache, route smaller models for retrieval-heavy paths
